How to Invest

Eswatini Capital Market Structure

A stockbroker is a financial professional who facilitates the buying and selling of securities, such as stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments, on behalf of clients. Their primary role is to connect investors with the stock market. Here are the key functions and responsibilities of a stockbroker:

  1. Executing Trades: Stockbrokers act as intermediaries between buyers and sellers in the stock market. They execute buy or sell orders for their clients, either on stock exchanges or over-the-counter markets.
  2. Compliance and Regulations: Stockbrokers must ensure that all trades and investment advice comply with financial regulations and ethical standards. They are typically licensed and regulated by financial authorities.

Overall, stockbrokers play a crucial role in helping individuals and institutions navigate financial markets, make informed investment decisions, and achieve their financial goals.

Transfer Secretaries

Transfer Secretaries, also known as registrars, play a vital role in capital markets by acting as intermediaries between companies that issue securities and the investors who hold them. Their primary responsibilities revolve around managing the records of shareholders and ensuring the smooth administration of transactions related to securities.

  1. Maintaining Shareholder Records: Transfer secretaries maintain a register of a companyâ€™s shareholders or bondholders, tracking who owns what amount of securities at any given time.
  2. Dividend and Interest Payments: Transfer secretaries ensure timely and accurate payments of dividends or interest to shareholders and bondholders.
  3. Handling Corporate Actions: Transfer secretaries administer corporate actions like stock splits, mergers, and proxy voting.
